Support Information & Privacy Policy

About Fridge Inventory: Grocery Cost

Fridge Inventory: Grocery Cost is a simple app that helps you log groceries and visualize what’s in your fridge. You can record items with details such as ingredients, quantity, price, purchase date, and store, then review your fridge inventory and shopping history anytime.

When you log ingredients used for cooking, the app can update your remaining inventory and automatically calculate the ingredient cost per meal. You can also review spending trends with easy-to-read charts.

The app is designed to work without creating an account. By default, your records are stored locally on your device. A premium upgrade removes ads and unlocks additional features such as category editing, CSV export, and iCloud backup/restore.

Privacy Policy

1. Introduction

Fridge Inventory: Grocery Cost is an application for recording and reviewing grocery items, fridge inventory, and ingredient costs. The app is designed to work without any user account, and the data you enter is stored locally on your device by default.

2. Information We Collect

The app itself does not collect, store, or transmit any personally identifiable information on external servers operated by the developer. In particular, the app does not send the following data outside your device:

  • Grocery items you enter (ingredient names, quantities, and units)
  • Prices, purchase dates, and store names
  • Cooking logs (meal info, used ingredients, calculated costs)
  • Notes, memos, and custom category names
  • Charts and totals derived from your records
  • Any other records you create within the app

All records you create remain on your device unless you choose to export or back up. The developer cannot view or access your local data.

3. Exception: Google AdMob (Free Version Only)

The free version of Fridge Inventory: Grocery Cost uses Google AdMob to display advertisements. AdMob may automatically process certain data for purposes such as ad delivery, frequency capping, fraud prevention, and measurement. This can include example data such as:

  • Advertising Identifier (IDFA) or other device identifiers (depending on your settings)
  • Approximate location (for example, inferred from IP address)
  • Basic ad interaction data (such as impressions or clicks)
  • Device information (model, OS version, language, etc.)

These data are processed by Google or its partners, not directly by the developer of this app. For details, please see Google’s Privacy Policy: https://policies.google.com/privacy

If you upgrade to the premium version, in-app ads are removed from the app UI. However, Google’s SDK may still be present in the app binary.

4. Your Data and Local Storage

All data you enter in the app — including:

  • Shopping logs (items, quantities/units, prices, dates, store names)
  • Fridge inventory and remaining quantities
  • Cooking logs and calculated ingredient costs
  • Notes, photos you attach to records (if applicable), and settings
  • Categories (default or customized)

is stored only on your device’s local storage by default. This information is not automatically uploaded to any server operated by the developer.

5. Backups and Restore (iCloud / Files)

Fridge Inventory: Grocery Cost provides a backup feature that allows you to create backup files and save them using the system Files interface (for example, iCloud Drive or other storage locations).

  • Backup files are created on your device and saved to the location you choose via Apple’s Files app.
  • Backup files may contain your app data (shopping logs, cooking logs, settings, etc.).
  • The developer does not operate any server for backups and cannot access your backup files.

When you restore from a backup, existing data in the app may be overwritten. This operation is performed locally on your device.

6. CSV Export

The app may allow you to export your records (for example, cooking logs and costs) as a CSV file so you can analyze the data in tools like Excel.

  • CSV files are generated on your device.
  • You choose where to save or share the exported file via the iOS share sheet.
  • The developer does not receive your exported data.

7. Photos and Photo Library Permissions

Depending on your usage, the app may allow you to save cooking photos to your photo library. If you use this feature, iOS may ask for permission to add photos to your library (Photo Library Add access).

  • Photo Library (Add Only) – used only to save images you choose to export to your photo library.

The app does not upload your photos to any server operated by the developer.

8. Permissions

Depending on your usage, Fridge Inventory: Grocery Cost may request access to:

  • Files / iCloud Drive – when you choose to create or restore a backup, or export data via the system file picker.
  • Photo Library (Add Only) – when you choose to save cooking photos to your photo library.

These permissions are used only for the intended features within the app. The app does not upload your records to any external server operated by the developer.

9. In-App Purchases

Fridge Inventory: Grocery Cost may offer premium features via in-app purchases. All purchase processing is handled entirely by Apple’s App Store via StoreKit. The app does not access or store payment information such as credit card numbers.

10. Third-Party Services

The following third-party services may be used by the app:

  • Google AdMob – for displaying advertisements in the free version.
  • Apple StoreKit – for processing in-app purchases.
  • Apple iCloud / Files – when you choose to save or restore backup files using iCloud Drive or other locations via the Files app.
  • Apple Photos – when you choose to save images to your photo library.

These services may collect technical or advertising-related data under their own privacy policies. Please refer to each provider’s official documentation for detailed information.

11. Data Sharing

The app does not:

  • Sell user data to third parties
  • Share user-entered records outside the device (unless you choose to export/share)
  • Transfer your app data to external servers managed by the developer

12. Children’s Privacy

This app is not specifically directed at children under the age of 13. AdMob is configured as “Not designed for children.”
